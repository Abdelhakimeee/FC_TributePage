Nikola Tesla

The genius who electrified the world
Nikola Tesla, standing in his laboratory, demonstrates the principles of alternating current - part of his mission to bring electricity to every corner of the world.

Here's a time line of Dr.Nikola life:

  • 1847 - Born in Milan, Ohio, USA
  • 1854 - Family moves to Port Huron, Michigan
  • 1859 - Begins working as a trainboy on the Grand Trunk Railroad; sets up a small laboratory in one of the baggage cars
  • 1862 - Saves a station agent's child from being struck by a train, earning free telegraphy lessons in gratitude
  • 1863 - Starts working as a telegraph operator, which gives him access to more scientific experiments
  • 1868 - Moves to Boston and gets his first patent for an electric vote recorder, but it is not commercially successful
  • 1869 - Moves to New York City and invents the Universal Stock Ticker, which becomes a financial success
  • 1870 - Opens a workshop in Newark, New Jersey, and starts working on telegraph improvements
  • 1874 - Develops the quadruplex telegraph, capable of sending four messages simultaneously on one wire, making it highly valuable for Western Union
  • 1876 - Opens an industrial research laboratory in Menlo Park, New Jersey, considered one of the first of its kind
  • 1877 - Invents the phonograph, which astonishes the world and earns him the nickname "The Wizard of Menlo Park"
  • 1879 - Develops a practical and long-lasting electric light bulb, revolutionizing indoor lighting and marking a significant advancement in electricity use
  • 1882 - Opens the world's first central power station, the Pearl Street Station in New York City, providing electricity for part of Manhattan
  • 1884 - Wife Mary Stilwell dies; Edison is left to care for their three children
  • 1886 - Marries Mina Miller, with whom he has three more children
  • 1887 - Moves to West Orange, New Jersey, and establishes a larger research laboratory, where he will invent many more devices
  • 1891 - Patents the motion picture camera (kinetograph), contributing to the foundation of the film industry
  • 1892 - General Electric is formed by merging Edison General Electric and Thomson-Houston Company, but Edison no longer controls the company
  • 1901 - Works on iron ore separation, though the project is ultimately unsuccessful
  • 1911 - Consolidates all his companies into Thomas A. Edison, Inc.
  • 1915 - Named honorary president of the Naval Consulting Board, which advises the U.S. Navy during World War I
  • 1920 - Establishes the Motion Picture Patents Company to monopolize the film industry, though it ultimately fails
  • 1928 - Receives a special gold medal from the U.S. Congress for his contributions to science and industry
  • 1931 - Dies at the age of 84 in West Orange, New Jersey; the nation dims its lights in his honor
